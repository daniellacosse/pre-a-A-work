MY LAST HEAVEN is an action RPG which consistently tests the user's ability to react to stimuli in combat through the use of the AP (Action-Potential) Interface. Not only does this prevent the gamer from having to memorize complex controls, but it allows the developer to keep the experience fresh and exciting.

This constant stream of commands are conveyed to the user in the form of impulses, which can either be voluntary or involuntary. Involuntary impulses occur in combat, generally in response to an enemies attack. Voluntary impulses can be triggered by the player while Louis is running about, allowing them to have a level of control over the battle flow.

These Impulses are managed through the previously mentioned API, stripped down to its basic elements;

As you can imagine, the impulses are released from the emitter on the right end of the API and race down the axon in order to give the player a moment to register the command list. Once the impulse enters the neural well, it is subject to the player's response. The closer the player's response is to the dendrite in the center of the well determines the DP (dendrite proximity) rewards and penalties that are attributed with Louis' elicited response.

A sequence of quality player responses increases the SP (Synaptic Progress) meter, the grey circle in the center of the neural well, and the damage multiplier, the number located above the cleft. It's maximum factor is 12... good luck getting there.

When the SP meter is full, Louis can activate one of his synapses, a powerful ability which deals above-average damage. They are unlocked throughout the course the game as you customise Louis to your personal preferences.

Dendrite proximity is determined by the labeled circles located within the neural well. If an involuntary impulse is activiated within the following circle, the DP consequences (DPCs) are as follows;

Time at Activation Effectiveness SP Increase Damage Multiplier Increase
Not at all: Action will fail. None. Damage Multiplier will go to 0.
At or within Circle 1: Gives the action a chance to occur. Slight. None.
At or within Circle 2: The action will most likely occur and may be extra effective. Some. Adds +1 to the combo.
At or within Circle 3: The action will occur and will most likely be extra effective. Noticeable. Adds +3 to the combo.
Bull's Eye: The action will occur and will be very effective. Large. Increases by x0.25

(For each 10 in the combo, the damage multiplier increases by x0.25)

The following is a list of Louis' base statistics.

Threshold:Your mental resillience; you'll flatline if you don't keep your threshold high.

Energy: Fuels your projections, or the manifestations of Louis' feelings.

Potency: Directly affects damage output. More potency, more damage.
Instinct: How fast you notice things and react to them. A high instinct level allows you to react to things you couldn't react to before, increases the time that impulses spend in the API, and gives you more of a cushion when it comes to DP rewards and penalties.
Combat Control: How well you can control your actions and emotions; more combat control will ensure that DP rewards give higher hit rates.

Creative Control: How well you can manage your creative power. Higher creative control increases efficiency in combat, and is critical in preventing your creative powers from getting out of hand.
Intimidation: The style in which you approach your enemies and vice versa. If your intimidation is just below the opponent's intimidation, you'll do less damage. If it is far below the opponent's intimidation, you'll freeze in fear or run away.
Faith: Increases your ability to find yourself in desireable situations. A high faith level will increase things like item drops, rare encounters, and critical hit chance.

Creativity: A critical gameplay element which allows Louis to do experience more and more of his own imagination as his definition of reality fades away. Creativity is ranked from 0 to 7, 0 setting the game in a state of absolute realism, where 7 essentially grants Louis a weath of possibilities, anything that he (i.e. the development team) can concieve of.

Now it's time to cover voluntary impulses with more detail:

There are two types of voluntary impulses; synapses and projections. Projections are, more or less, spells, and come in seven "emotional" flavors; Despair, Anger, Fear, Courage, Joy, Pride and Logic.

When using a projection or synapse, the game's action pauses twice; once to allow you to make your selection and a second to select your targets. In between these pauses is the volunteered seqeuence of commands which allow you to use the ability. Like the involuntary DPCs, voluntary DPCs determine effectiveness of the attack, but cumutively rather than individually, and have no effect on the SP meter or the damage multiplier.

Synapses are special abilites which require a full SP meter to trigger. What synapses are available to you is dependent upon how you've been customising Louis...

Neurotransmitters are somewhat uncommon, but can be found everywhere. Once uncovered, they can be used to pave Louis' neural network, which is accessible in the game's menu. Louis' neural network is a multi-layered labyrinth of connected nerve clusters and cells, just waiting to be stimulated during Louis' travels. The cells give different bonuses based on the neurotransmitters which Louis uses to stimulate them;

Endorphin or an Orange N.transmitter - Activating a neuron with a orange n.transmitter usually grants a threshold or faith bonus.

Epinephrine or a Purple N.transmitter - Activating a neuron with a purple n.transmitter usually grants an energy or instinct bonus..

Acetylcholine or a Blue N.transmitter - Activating a neuron with a blue n.transmitter usually grants a control bonus..

GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid) or a Red N.transmitter - Activating a neuron with a red n.transmitter usually grants an intimidation or potency bonus.

An Amnesiaic or Clear N.transmitter - Use this on an already activated nerve cluster to clear it. These are very rare and potent, and can even increase creativity.

Of course, the game will let you know beforehand specifically what effect will come from stimulating a certain nerve cell. You can either choose to stimulate cells individually (each cell requires 1 neruotransmitter to be stimulated) or in clusters (each cluster requires 1 neruotransmitter for each cell in it to be stimulated) which take longer to save up for and reduce customization, but grant extra bonuses in bulk and twoard synapses.

The compound cluster is third and final type of object in Louis' neural network. These require a specific combination of neurotransmitters to be activated and cannot be split into nerve cells. When activated, they grand Louis an ability or projection which he can use in combat.

Synapses are unlocked based on the sequences of neural activations within Louis' nerual network, for instance, if there are 5 red transmitters in a row, Louis gains the synapse "Fury Fist." There is an in-game "synapse hunter" which aids the player in their hunt for useful synapses.

The Dynamic Equip System (DES) reffers to the manner in which Louis’ equipment changes gradually throughout the game based on the player’s play style (and Louis’ creativity). It also gets stronger as he gets stronger. He begins with a dyn. One-hander and a partial suit. He eventually fills out the suit, gets another one-hander, a two-hander, a shield, and a set of ranged weapons (two one-handed, one two-handed).

A simple example of equipment dynamics; If the player deals large amounts of damage in combat, but has no sense of self preservation, then the modeling algorithm begins to make Louis’ attire appear more jagged, even serrated. The statistical algorithm then adjusts the bonuses so that the armor gives more power and less threshold.

Static Loot is also obtained from enemies for two reasons: to equip to memories and dreams (party members) and to allow the player to change their play style abruptly (with high enough creativity, suits of equipment can be changed instantly in mid-combat) by giving them an opportunity to equip something that fits their current needs more effectively.
