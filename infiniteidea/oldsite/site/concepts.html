The following are a series of ideas I'm currently developing, hence the name of the site. Bear in mind that all ideas are in progress and that they're all the intellectual property of Daniel LaCosse. I'm hoping to, at some point in the distant future, expand this section to allow users to post their own content (under certain legal conditions, of course) so that I don't feel like such a dick by hogging this entire site to myself.

  

Algàra is a mysterious unknown country which has laid beneath the sands of Algeria since before Algeria became known as what it is today. The residents of this vast intellectual society have managed to unnoticed by the modern world until only recently, when CIA agents discover technology thought to be beyond the scope of modern humans.

The entire history of Algàra, its rise and fall, whould be chonicled in two playable games - a vast MMO and a hack 'n' slash RPG with online capabilies.

Thusfar, only scraps of the country's complex language are available for viewing.

 

Ventures in an Unstable Mind chronciles the final days of a schizophrenic parapalegic who finds difficulty in any form of communication other than poetry. His fractured reality spins around him without warning, hopefully strip the viewer of, by the end of the work, any kind of sense of realism. It is a exploration of the human psyche, questions if reality truly is more defined than fantasy, and even transcends onto a plane of universal subconcious, the plane that Mr. Carl oversees in My Last Heaven. It is a story of misguided love, that the main character's determined chase of his soulmate into the afterlife is actually a frenzied search for self-meaning within his final moments.

Most of all, it is what you make it. That is most important.

 

 Part Psychonauts, Part DDR, Part Kingdom Hearts, MY LAST HEAVEN is a game which takes place in the same world as Ventures. It begins with the hero, a teenager named Louis, finding himself trapped within a coma. In his prison he is confronted by the mysterious Mr. Carl, purveyor of the Collective Unconcious. Mr. Carl's job, in the grander scheme of things, is to, from time to time, select individuals to remotely run tests on, helping to asses the current strength of humanity and the Collective Unconcious. Louis later finds he is chosen by Mr. Carl because Mr. Carl feels Louis is inable to pass the test, treating Louis like any of his more mature experimentees. Why Mr. Carl seems to be sabotaging the fate of the Collective Unconcious is unkown to Louis, and in order for him to find out, he must first survive the horrors of his own psyche.
